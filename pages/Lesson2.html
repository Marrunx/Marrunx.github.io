The 19th Century

the birth of Modernity, the Age of Revolution.-new modes of production, transportation, and communication were introduced. The flow of work progression shifted from manual labor to machine-based.

during the 19th century, Spain was experiencing a slow economic decline.

Jose Rizal lived throughout the nineteenth century, an era of Philippine history. That period saw significant economic and social transformation, as well as the creation of our nation.

This century brought a significant transition from the preceding. Spain's monopolistic policies resulted in decades of economic stagnation. Foreign consulates were created prior to and following 1834. Mail Services The relationship between Manila and Cavite dates back to 1839.

As a result of the great economic transformation in the life of the Filipino, a middle class (class media) of Asian and Eurasian mestizos emerged in the Philippine social pyramid.

This class emerged from the economic boom derived from expanded agriculture and commerce embarked on by the rising native entrepreneurs.

  • Top: Peninsulares- pure-blooded Spaniards, born in Spain.
  • Second to highest: pure-blooded Spaniards, but were not born in Spain.
  • Middle: Mestizos- mixed-race, especially one having Spanish and indigenous race.
  • Bottom: Indios- or “indigenta”. The native Filipinos are considered as the lowest level in class society.

Key Factors in the Emergence of Nationalism in the late 19th Century.

> The rapid spread of education
> Reform of education such as preferring skilled labor more than dependent on prayers
> The Educational Decree of 1863, mandates to have at least one primary school for each local town
> The return of Jesuits, bearing new ideas and methods for the educational system.
> By 1866, literate people in the Philippines were higher than in Spain.
